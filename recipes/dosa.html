Dosa

Description:

A popular South Indian dish made from fermented rice and lentil batter, cooked until crispy and golden-brown.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup split black lentils (urad dal)
  • Water (as needed)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

Steps:

  1. Wash the rice and urad dal separately and soak them in water for about 4-6 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the water and grind the rice and dal separately to a smooth paste using a little water as needed.
  3. Mix both the ground pastes together, add salt, and mix well. Let the batter ferment overnight or for about 8-10 hours.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or dosa pan over medium heat. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the pan and spread it evenly in a circular motion to make a thin dosa.
  5. Drizzle some oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa and cook until the bottom turns golden-brown and crisp.
  6. Flip the dosa and cook the other side for a minute or until it turns light golden brown.
  7. Repeat the process with the remaining batter to make more dosas.
  8. Serve hot dosas with coconut chutney, sambar, or your favorite side dish.
